Intro

I am a Senior at DigiPen Institute of Technology , graduating in April 2024 with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. My passion lies in building efficient game engine tools that will aid in the development of fun and engaging games.

During my time at DigiPen, I have been involved in many team projects. I also worked on several personal projects alongside them. Check them out here.

Team Projects

Online Multiplayer Game (01/2024 - 04/2024)

Project: 3D Multiplayer First Person Shooter
Engine: Unreal Engine 5.3 (Blueprints)
Role(s): Combat Programmer
Features Implemented: Weapon System, Health System, Player Death Functionality
Team Size: 4

My focus with this project was to build a weapon system that was robust, expandable, and modular. As such, I approached the development of the system with generalization in mind, aiming to be easy to use and/or modify by designers and programmers alike. The benefit of this system was that the team was able to iterate quickly after every playtest, and as a result, widen our scope for the final product.

Find the release here.

The Drawer War (08/2024 - 12/2024)

Project: Tech Demo / Research Project
Engine: Unity (C#)
Role(s): Systems Designer and AI Programmer
Features Implemented: AI Framework
Team Size: 2

What originally was meant to be a 2D bullet hell game featuring AI behaviors and physics simulations quickly pivoted into a tech demo / research project about Unity's ECS package. The tech demo still features several AI behaviors and a collision engine, but the focus was optimizing these features for performance. The AI framework I developed takes advantage of abstraction to modularize the different enemies and behaviors, while simultaneously reaping the full benefits of the data oriented nature of the ECS system. The result was a system that performs twice as fast as MonoBehaviors.

Find the repository here.

InfeStation (08/2022 - 12/2023)

Project: 3D Space Themed Horror First Person Shooter
Engine: Unreal Engine 5.0 (Blueprints)
Role(s): AI Programmer & Animations Programmer
Features Implemented: AI Framework, Main Enemy, Player & Enemy Animations
Team Size: 8

At the start of this project I had an interest in AI, so I decided to focus on developing AI behavior using UE5's behavior trees. Being my first time working with blueprints and behavior trees, I had insurmountable hurdles to overcome. In particular, the steep learning curve of UE's blueprint workflow and the sophisticated nature of the built in AI framework proved to be challenging. However, by prioritizing sandboxing and learning the ins and outs of the tasks I would implement I quickly learned how to work efficiently in this new environment. The end result was an organized system that made it easy to add and remove enemy behaviors as well as iterate on existing features.

Note that the version of this game that I lasted work on is not the latest version of the game. When the team decided to pursue a third semester of work for polish, I decided to leave the team in favor of starting a new project (TheDrawerWar). The AI programmer that took over my position continued to work on the enemy in the new release. Both releases are included below.

Find the old release and the new release here.

Rekindled Flame (08/2021 - 04/2022)

Project: 2D Horror Platformer
Engine: Custom Engine (C++)
Role(s): Physics & Collisions Programmer, Player Behaviors Programmer, Support Programmer
Features Implemented: Physics System, Player Movement Behaviors, Custom Imgui Editor
Team Size: 11

This was the project that birthed my passion for game tools development. Building a custom 2D engine was incredibly fun just as it was challenging. During the early stages of the engines development I was tasked with building the physics and collision systems. Later on I acted as a support programmer, assisting other team members with higher priority tasks in order to increase the team's overall productivity. As a support programmer I implemented player behavior features, a particle system, and helped implement an in-game custom level editor using imgui. The development of the editor was my personal highlight of this project. Building systems that both encapsulate the complexities of the underlying feature as well as simplify the work done by designers was what made me realize my passion for developing game tools.

Find the release here.

About

Hello! My name is Mahin Arjhuna Goban and I am a game tools programmer. I am 24 years old and I come from Malaysia. Ever since I was young I have been playing video games, and so my love for video games grew as I did. Some of the games that highlighted my childhood and teenage years were League of Legends, Osu, and Overwatch. In particular, I have been playing League of Legends and Osu for over 10 years.

As a programmer, I enjoy tackling challenging problems using creative ideas. I also really enjoy the abstraction of higher level concepts, building structured frameworks, and modularizing code. As such, my goal of becoming a game engine tools developer is fitting.
