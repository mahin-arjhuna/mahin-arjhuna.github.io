Intro

I am an aspiring Tools Programmer, having graduated from DigiPen Institute of Technology in April 2024 with a Bachelor of Science in Computer Science. My passion lies in building efficient game engine tools that will aid in the development of fun and engaging games.

During my time at DigiPen, I have been involved in many team projects. I also worked on several personal projects alongside them. Check them out here.

Academic Projects

Online Multiplayer Game (01/2024 - 04/2024)

Project: 3D Multiplayer First Person Shooter
Engine: Unreal Engine 5.3 (Blueprints)
Role(s): Weapons and Tools Programmer
Features Implemented: Weapon System, Health System, Player Death Functionality
Team Size: 4

My focus with this project was to build a weapon system that was robust, expandable, and modular. As such, I approached the development of the system with generalization in mind, aiming for ease of use and/or modification by designers and programmers alike. As a result the team was able to iterate quickly after every playtest and widen our scope for the final product. Additionally, while working closely with the networking programmer, I managed to pick up replication techniques in blueprints.

This project is still in progress, therefore there is no release link.

The Drawer War (08/2024 - 12/2024)

Project: Tech Demo / Research Project
Engine: Unity (C#)
Role(s): Framework and AI Programmer
Features Implemented: AI Framework
Team Size: 2

What was originally meant to be a 2D bullet hell game featuring AI movement behaviors and physics simulations quickly pivoted into a tech demo / research project about Unity's ECS package. Our focus then shifted to implementing a few AI behaviors and a collision engine, optimizing these features for performance. The AI framework I developed takes advantage of abstraction to modularize the different enemies and behaviors, while simultaneously reaping the full benefits of the data oriented nature of the ECS system. This resulted in a system twice as fast as MonoBehaviors.

Find the repository here.

Perlin Noise Procedural Generation (12/2024)

Project: Tech Demo / Research Project
Engine: Unity (C#)
Role(s): Framework Programmer
Features Implemented: Grid Framework
Team Size: 3

During an AI class I took at Digipen, our final project was a simple tech demo of a chosen topic. Myself and a team of two others decided on Perlin Nouse and Procedural Generation. Given only a week to complete the demo, we focused on a simple medium of 2D tiles with corners that could either represent land (green) or water (blue). My focus was on creating a Grid struct and designing and implementing a simple framework that would connect the tile assets to the Perlin Noise generator, resulting in a procedurally generated 2D terrain.

InfeStation (08/2022 - 12/2023)

Project: 3D Space Themed Horror First Person Shooter
Engine: Unreal Engine 5.0 (Blueprints)
Role(s): AI and Animations Programmer
Features Implemented: AI Framework, Main Enemy, Player & Enemy Animations
Team Size: 8

My interest in artificial intelligence motivated me to focus on developing AI behaviors using UE5's behavior trees. It was my first time working with blueprints and behavior trees and as such, I had insurmountable hurdles to overcome. The steep learning curve of UE's blueprint workflow and the sophisticated nature of the built in AI framework in particular, proved to be challenging. However, by prioritizing sandboxing and learning the ins and outs of the tasks I would implement, I quickly learned how to work efficiently in this new environment. The end result was an organized system that made it easy to add and remove enemy behaviors as well as iterate on existing features.

Note that the version of this game that I last worked on is not the latest. When the team decided to pursue a third semester of work for polish, I decided to leave the team in favor of starting a new project (TheDrawerWar). The AI programmer who took over my position continued to work on the enemy in the new release. Both releases are included below.

Find the old release and the new release here.

Rekindled Flame (08/2021 - 04/2022)

Project: 2D Horror Platformer
Engine: Custom Engine (C++)
Role(s): Physics & Collisions Programmer, Player Behaviors Programmer, Support Programmer
Features Implemented: Physics System, Player Movement Behaviors, Custom Imgui Editor
Team Size: 11

This was the project that ignited my passion for game tools development. Building a custom 2D engine was incredibly fun just as it was challenging. During the early stages of the engines development, I was tasked with building the physics and collision systems. Later on I acted as a support programmer, assisting other team members with higher priority tasks in order to increase the team's overall productivity. As a support programmer I implemented player behavior features, a particle system, and helped implement an in-game custom level editor using imgui. The development of the editor was the highlight of this project for me. Building systems capable of encapsulating the complexities of the underlying feature as well as simplifing the work done by potential users of the system was what made me realize my passion for developing tools.

Tic-Tac-Toe w/ Minimax

Language: Java

While learning programming for the first time in Python, I programmed a console tic-tac-toe game. Being the most challenging task at the time, I wanted to eventually improve on it. So, a few years of programming experience later, I decided to recreate my tic-tac-toe program in Java, the language I was learning at the time. I settled on adding a GUI using Java swing, and implementing some kind of AI. After some research, I decided to implement minimax since it was conceptually simple to code but also complex enough in its behavior.

About

Hello! My name is Mahin Arjhuna Goban and I am a Tools Programmer. I am a 24 year old Malaysian. I have been playing video games from a young age, and my love for video games grew as I did. Some of the games which defined my childhood and teenage years were League of Legends, Osu, and Overwatch. I have been playing League of Legends and Osu for over 10 years, and I have competed in Osu regional tournaments for 5 years (2015-2018, 2022). I made it quite far up the global and national ladder, achieving a peak rank of 7K / 5M globally, and 30 / 10K in Malaysia.

I play sports, in particular soccer and badminton. I enjoyed soccer so much that I joined the official DigiPen soccer team and participated in local competitions. I also like indoor climbing from time to time.

Music has always been an important part of my life. I learned the drums when I was 10 years old, then moved onto the guitar. I currently enjoy creating tracks on FL Studio and uploading some of them to soundcloud. I use soundcloud as a sort of playground for me to experiment with music creation and keep track of my progress. Osu, which is incidentally a music rhythm game, is the game I excel at. What I love most most about Osu is the fact that it provides a means to visualize an auditory experience. When done well it can be an absolutely amazing experience.

As a programmer I love tackling challenging problems using creative ideas. I also really enjoy the abstraction of higher level concepts, building structured frameworks, and modularizing code. As such, my goal of becoming a tools developer is fitting.

As a person I value good friends and enjoy deep conversations. I also care about making meaningful connections and spending time with the people I care about.
